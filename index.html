   2023 International Symposium on Microphysiological Systems （MPS 2023）and the 6th Academic Conference and Industrial Investment Forum on Organoid and Organ-on-Chip will be held in Nanjing, China from November 10th to 13th, 2023. The conference is jointly organized by Southeast University and Columbia University. Esteemed experts, scholars, and entrepreneurs engaged in research and application in the fields of organ-on-chip, organoid, tissue engineering, biomedical big data, and related areas from domestic and international universities, research institutions, biomedical engineering companies, and other organizations are cordially invited to participate in in-depth discussions, exchange ideas, and explore the latest research directions in the field of biomedical engineering, including organ-on-chips, organoids, and biomedical big data. The conference aims to showcase the latest scientific achievements, establish a platform for collaboration between research institutions and biomedical engineering companies, and collectively promote the development of organ-on-chip research and industry in China. The first five conferences have invited renowned experts in the field of organ-on-chip and tissue engineering, both internationally and domestically, including David Weitz, Dan Tagle, Kam Leong, Marcus Textor, Michael Shuler, Kevin Healy, Gordana Vunjak-Novakovic and Zhongze Gu.

   We sincerely welcome scholars, representatives from the business community, and graduate students engaged in biomedical engineering research to actively submit their papers and participate in the conference!

Conference Theme (Provisional)

1. Construction of Organoid and Organ-on-Chip:

   Organ-on-chip design and fabrication, 3D bioprinting, organoid culture, microfluidic chip design and manufacturing, stem cells and tissue engineering, biomaterials and bio-inspired materials, etc.

2. Measurement and Analysis of Organoid and Organ-on-Chip：

   Cell and tissue imaging, cellular and tissue mechanics measurement, biosensing materials and devices, biomedical big data and bioinformatics.；

3. Applications of Organoid and Organ-on-Chip：

  Animal alternatives, toxicology, pharmacology, drug screening, personalized diagnostics and therapeutics, space applications, environmental assessment, etc.

4. Organ-on-Chip and AI 5. Organ-on-Chip and Precision Medicine 6. Standardization and Industrialization of Organ-on-Chip

    • Author Guidelines

  • Submissions to the conference should consist of original abstracts or papers written in English. Authors' innovation and achievements should be highlighted, demonstrating significant academic or application value.The purpose of this conference is to enhance communication, and in respect of the majority of authors' preferences, the conference proceedings will be compiled and shared in electronic format.Therefore, papers presented at the conference may still be submitted to other conferences or journals without any conflict (there is no copyright transfer issue with this conference). Authors are requested to strictly adhere to the provided template and formatting requirements, preparing their submissions in PDF format. Please submit the paper along with the conference registration form(Attachment 2)to the conference committee via email (zhouwei@i-bmd.org).For more details, refer to the "Submission Template and Guidelines".Please ensure that the first author and corresponding author's names and contact information (affiliation, phone number, email address) are mentioned in the footer of the paper's first page. Please indicate the conference theme to which the paper belongs.
  • After the review process, selected papers will be chosen for oral presentations or poster presentations, and acceptance notifications will be sent via email.
  • The organizing committee will select one first prize, two second prizes, three third prizes, and several honorable mentions from the oral presentations.
